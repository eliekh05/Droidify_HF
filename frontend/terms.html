Terms of Use

Read this before you flash anything. It is short and direct.

What Droidify is

Droidify is a free, read-only index. It fetches publicly available information about Android ROMs, recoveries, root tools, and devices from third-party sources and displays it in one place. It does not host, distribute, modify, or own any firmware, ROM, or recovery file. Every download link goes directly to the project that made it.

Knox, warranty, and Play Protect

Droidify is not responsible for a tripped Knox counter, a voided warranty, a failed Play Protect certification, or any consequence that follows from unlocking your bootloader or flashing unofficial software. Those outcomes are between you and your device. Droidify showed you that something exists and where to get it. What you do with that information is entirely your decision.

Samsung Knox trips the instant you unlock the bootloader. It is a hardware-level fuse. Once it reads 0x1 it reads 0x1 forever — no stock firmware flash, no Samsung service centre, no community trick, nothing changes it. If you are a Samsung user who cares about Knox, Samsung Pay, or Secure Folder, this is your only warning.

Google Play Protect certification works differently — it checks whether your device's software stack has been verified by Google. Rooting, flashing unsigned builds, or running uncertified ROMs can break certification and stop certain apps from running. If banking apps, streaming services, or work profiles stop working after you flash something, the conversation you need to have is with the ROM developer, not with us.

Manufacturer warranties are voided by bootloader unlocking on most devices. Some manufacturers make this reversible by re-locking, most do not. Check your device's documentation before you proceed, not after.

Data loss

Unlocking the bootloader wipes your device. Flashing a ROM wipes it again. Flashing the wrong partition table, the wrong file, or interrupting a flash mid-way can brick your device permanently. These are not edge cases — they are documented, expected consequences of the process you are choosing to start.

If you lost data: the lesson is to back up before flashing, not to look for someone to blame. Droidify does not have your data, did not touch your device, and cannot help you recover anything. The developer who built the ROM or recovery you flashed cannot magically recover your data either — the encryption keys were wiped when you formatted.

If your device is bricked: go to XDA Forums, the ROM's Telegram group, or the GitHub repository of the recovery or ROM you used. The people who built it are the ones who know how to unbrick it. That is where your energy belongs, not here.

ROM, recovery, and firmware files

Every file linked through Droidify was uploaded by its developer or a community maintainer to their own infrastructure — GitHub Releases, SourceForge, OrangeFox's CDN, TWRP's servers, or their own website. Droidify did not upload it, did not sign it, and does not verify it beyond what a scraper reading a public page can determine.

If a file is corrupt, contains unexpected content, bricked your device, or otherwise caused you harm — the person who uploaded it is the one who can help you. Find them on XDA, their GitHub profile, or their Telegram channel. That is where the file came from. That is where the conversation needs to happen. Droidify is not that person, does not speak for that person, and cannot act on their behalf.

Always verify the SHA256 checksum of any file against what the developer published before you flash it. Every link on Droidify shows the source name so you know exactly where you are going.

GitHub issues

The Droidify GitHub issue tracker is for bugs in the indexer and missing data sources. It is not a support forum for device problems.

Issues opened about any of the following will be automatically closed by a bot with no further response:

  • Tripped Knox counter or Knox warranty void
  • Voided manufacturer warranty
  • Failed Google Play Protect certification
  • Data loss from flashing
  • Bricked device
  • Requests to recover data or un-brick a device
  • Complaints about a ROM, recovery, or firmware file that Droidify linked to

These are not things Droidify can help with, has any responsibility for, or will discuss. The bot closes them immediately to save everyone's time. If your issue falls into one of those categories, go to the developer who made the file — they are the right person to talk to.
